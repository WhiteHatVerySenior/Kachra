Geeks Corner

Why this training helps at scale.

Back to Game

Behavior change at household level

  • Makes segregation rules simple and memorable (wet/dry/hazard/e-waste).
  • Repetition builds habit; wrong-bin penalties simulate real consequences.
  • Helps families teach kids and elders with a shared, low-effort activity.

Cleaner waste streams

  • Less contamination = higher recycling and compost rates.
  • Better segregation reduces rejection/landfill burden.
  • Dry waste becomes valuable instead of useless.

Public health + safety

  • Sanitary/hazardous waste kept out of general streams.
  • Fewer injuries for sanitation workers (blades, broken glass, diapers).

Municipal cost reduction

  • Sorting at source reduces manual sorting costs at MRFs.
  • Lower transport and processing cost when waste is separated.

Education at scale

  • A simple, mobile-friendly game spreads faster than workshops.
  • Can be reused in schools, RWAs, campaigns, and CSR programs.

Data-driven improvement

  • With score tracking, you can see where misconceptions persist.
  • Helps design better awareness campaigns.
